# Statement of Facts (SOF) — Analysis Guide

## What Is a Statement of Facts?

A Statement of Facts (SOF) is a chronological record of events during a vessel's port call, maintained by the ship's agent (or sometimes the master) and typically countersigned by the master and the charterer's representative. It is the primary evidentiary document for laytime calculation and demurrage claims. A well-kept SOF records every significant event, timestamped to the minute.

## Typical SOF Entries

| Entry | Significance |
|---|---|
| Vessel arrived pilot station / anchorage | Start of port call; relevant to arrived-ship status |
| NOR tendered | Triggers laytime mechanism; record exact time |
| NOR accepted / acknowledged | May determine NOR effectiveness under some clauses |
| Received free pratique | Condition for legal readiness (unless WIFPON) |
| Inward customs cleared | Condition for legal readiness (unless WICCON) |
| Vessel shifted from anchorage | Begin inward passage |
| First line made fast / all fast | Vessel berthed; relevant for berth charterparties |
| Hatch covers opened / beams removed | Practical readiness for operations |
| Commenced loading / discharging | Cargo work begins |
| Operations ceased — rain / swell | Weather exception; record time weather started |
| Operations resumed | Weather exception ends |
| Breakdown of [ship's/shore's] crane | Note whose equipment failed — determines risk |
| Awaiting gang / labor | Charterer's responsibility |
| Shift change / gang break | Port custom; typically short periods not excluded |
| Completed loading / discharging | Operations end |
| Hatches closed / secured | Final act of cargo phase |
| Last line let go | Vessel departs |
| Documents signed / presented | May affect completion time |

## The Laytime Analysis Sequence

When reviewing a SOF to calculate laytime, apply the following checks **in order** for each time interval:

### Step 1: Has a Valid NOR Been Tendered?

Before any laytime can run, the NOR must have been tendered and must be effective.

- Check: Was the vessel an arrived ship at the time of tender? (Port vs berth charterparty; WIBON?)
- Check: Was the vessel physically and legally ready? (WICCON/WIFPON applicable?)
- Check: Was NOR given to the correct party (charterers or their agent)?
- Check: Was it in writing?
- **If NOR was not validly tendered, no laytime runs until a valid NOR is given.**

### Step 2: When Does NOR Become Effective?

- Apply WIBON: If present, NOR is effective upon tender even at anchorage.
- Apply berth charterparty rule: Without WIBON, NOR only becomes effective when vessel is all fast at berth.
- Apply acceptance clauses: Does the charter party require charterers to formally accept NOR? If so, when was it accepted?

### Step 3: Apply Notice Period / Turn Time

- From the NOR effective time, add the contractual turn time (e.g., 12 hours, 24 hours, 6 hours).
- Check whether the turn time runs through Sundays/holidays (some charter parties exclude S&H from the notice period calculation — read carefully).
- Check whether "office hours" restrictions affect when the notice period starts.
- **Laytime commencement = NOR effective time + turn time (adjusted for any restrictions).**

### Step 4: For Each Interval in the SOF, Determine Whether Time Counts

Once laytime has commenced, go through the SOF interval by interval and ask:

**4a. Is this period a SHEX exception?**
- Is it a Sunday or public holiday?
- Check the relevant holiday calendar for the port in question.
- If SHEX EIU: Did actual cargo operations take place? If yes, the time counts despite being a holiday.
- If SHEX UU: Does not count regardless.

**4b. Is there a weather exception (WWD)?**
- Did the weather actually prevent cargo operations during this interval?
- Was there a weather stoppage recorded in the SOF?
- Is there evidence that weather was the *cause* (not a coincidental concurrent condition)?
- Prorate if weather stopped a portion of a period.

**4c. Is there another express exclusion?**
- Breakdown of vessel's equipment (ship's risk — exclude from laytime).
- Strike of labor (check whose labor — usually charterers' risk for shore labor strikes).
- Any other contractual exclusion clause.

**4d. Is this period before operations began but after laytime commenced?**
- Time counts even if cargo operations have not yet started — laytime is not conditional on operations commencing, only on commencement of the laytime period.

**4e. Apply the "once on demurrage, always on demurrage" principle.**
- Once laytime is exhausted, demurrage runs. SHEX does not suspend demurrage unless the charter party expressly provides ("SHEX on demurrage" clause).

### Step 5: Sum All Running Laytime and Compare to Allowed Laytime

- Allowed laytime = quantity ÷ daily rate (or as fixed in the charter party).
- Running laytime = sum of all periods that counted.
- If running laytime > allowed laytime → **DEMURRAGE** due to charterers.
- If running laytime < allowed laytime → **DESPATCH** due to shipowners (if clause provides for it).

### Step 6: Calculate the Demurrage or Despatch Amount

- **Demurrage** = (Running laytime − Allowed laytime) × Demurrage rate
- **Despatch** = (Allowed laytime − Running laytime) × Despatch rate
- Express result in days and hours, then apply the daily rate.

## Common SOF Disputes and How to Resolve Them

### NOR tendered but vessel not yet in berth (berth charterparty, no WIBON)
- NOR is premature and ineffective.
- Laytime does not commence until vessel is all fast at berth.
- Check whether charterers' agents endorsed/accepted the NOR regardless — may be estoppel argument.

### NOR tendered during night / outside office hours
- Check charter party: "office hours" restriction?
- If restricted, NOR may only take effect at start of next office day.
- If WIBON with 6-hour notice: does notice period start running at night?

### Overlapping exceptions (weather + SHEX)
- If weather stops operations on a Sunday (SHEX), and the charter party is WWDSHEX:
  - The Sunday already doesn't count (SHEX).
  - The weather stoppage doesn't add further benefit — the day is already excluded.
  - No "double exclusion" — you cannot claim both simultaneously.

### SOF not countersigned by charterers' representative
- An unsigned SOF is still evidence, but may be disputed.
- The master's record and the agent's port log are supporting documents.
- Any protest letters or email correspondence may substitute.

### Rain recorded but operations continued
- If the SOF says "rain" but does not say "operations stopped — rain", time continues to count.
- The weather must actually stop operations; mere rain that operations continue through is not a WWD exception.
