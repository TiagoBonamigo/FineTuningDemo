# BIMCO Charter Party Templates, Standard Forms, and Rider Clauses

## Overview

The Baltic and International Maritime Council (BIMCO) publishes standard voyage charter party forms used across the dry bulk, tanker, and specialized cargo trades. These forms contain pre-printed laytime and demurrage provisions that are often modified by rider clauses negotiated between shipowners and charterers.

## Major BIMCO and Industry Standard Forms

### GENCON 94 (General Purpose Voyage Charter Party)
The most widely used general-purpose dry cargo voyage charter. Box layout with a standard printed form plus rider clauses appended. Key laytime provisions:

- **Box 15**: Laytime for loading.
- **Box 16**: Laytime for discharging.
- **Box 19**: Demurrage rate.
- **Box 20**: Cancelling date.
- **Clause 6**: Laytime commencement provisions. Under GENCON 94, the standard form provides that laytime commences when the vessel is in berth and NOR has been given, unless modified by WIBON or similar rider.
- **Clause 7**: Demurrage/Despatch clause. Demurrage at the rate in Box 19; despatch at half demurrage rate unless otherwise agreed.

Common GENCON rider modifications:
- Adding WIBON, WICCON, WIFPON to enable NOR at anchorage.
- Specifying WWDSHEX EIU (GENCON is silent on weather by default).
- Adding a turn time clause (e.g., "laytime to commence 12 hours after NOR").
- Adding "once on demurrage, always on demurrage" language.

### ASBATANKVOY (Tanker Voyage Charter Party — American)
Standard form for tanker voyages, particularly in the US Gulf trade. Laytime regime:
- Laytime is calculated in **running hours** (not working days).
- NOR can be tendered at the loading/discharging port anchorage or, if vessel is in berth, upon all fast — whichever occurs first.
- Grace period (free time) of 6 hours for loading and 6 hours for discharging is typically included.
- Demurrage calculated on a per-hour basis (daily rate ÷ 24).
- Weather provisions: time lost due to weather counts under ASBATANKVOY (SHINC effectively applies by default for laytime calculation in many interpretations).

### NORGRAIN 89 (Norwegian Grain Charter Party)
Standard form for grain cargoes in the North Atlantic and Scandinavian trades. Key features:
- Separate laytime for loading and discharging.
- WWDSHEX applies unless otherwise specified.
- Turn time provisions built into the standard form.
- Specific provisions for strikes and ice clauses common in the form.

### SYNACOMEX (European Grain Charter Party)
Used in European grain trades. SHEX applies but EIU/UU must be specified in rider clauses. Contains specific provisions for berth availability and shifting.

### COAL-OREVOY (Coal and Ore Charter Party)
Used for bulk dry commodity trades (coal, iron ore). Provides for:
- Rate-based laytime (tonnes per day).
- WWD SHEX EIU as the common default.
- Weather provisions related to vessel trim and loading arm limitations.

### FERTIVOY 88 (Fertilizer Voyage Charter Party)
Designed for bulk fertilizer cargoes. Notable for:
- Strict cargo hold cleanliness requirements linked to laytime (vessel not deemed ready until holds pass inspection).
- Extended notice period clauses.

## How to Read Rider Clauses vs Printed Clauses

A charter party consists of:
1. **Printed standard terms** (the BIMCO form text).
2. **Boxes/recitals** (filled in at negotiation — the "particulars").
3. **Rider clauses** (additional typewritten or emailed clauses attached at the end).

**Principle**: In the event of conflict, rider clauses prevail over printed clauses, and handwritten amendments prevail over typewritten rider clauses. This is because later-added or specifically negotiated terms are presumed to reflect the true agreement of the parties.

**Practical consequence**: If a rider clause says "WIBON WICCON WIFPON" but the printed Clause 6 requires the vessel to be in berth before NOR can be given, the rider prevails. The vessel may tender NOR at anchorage.

## Recap Telex / Fixture Recap

Before the formal charter party is signed, the parties exchange a **Recap Telex** (or recap email) summarizing all agreed terms. The recap is contractually binding even before the full form is signed. For laytime purposes:

- The recap typically sets out: laytime allowed (or the rate and quantity to calculate it), demurrage/despatch rates, SHEX/SHINC/WWDSHEX, turn time, WIBON string, and any key rider clause amendments.
- If the printed form conflicts with the recap, the recap generally prevails (it is more specific and more recent).
- Always check whether the charter party as signed reflects the recap — discrepancies create disputes.

## Common Rider Clauses Affecting Laytime

### "WIPON/WIBON/WICCON/WIFPON" Rider
Allows NOR to be tendered before the vessel has reached the port, before berthing, before customs clearance, and before free pratique. This is the broadest NOR trigger string.

### "Turn Time / Notice Period" Rider
Example: "Laytime shall not commence before 06:00 hours on the day following the day NOR is tendered, if NOR is tendered after 12:00 hours noon on the previous day, or 06:00 hours on the same day if tendered before 12:00 hours noon."

### "Inward Passage" Rider
Example: "Time used in moving from anchorage to berth not to count as laytime." Or alternatively: "Time from weighing anchor to first line fast at berth to count as half laytime."

### "Hatch Inspection" Rider
Common in grain/fertilizer trades: "Laytime shall not commence until holds have been inspected and passed by the shipper's surveyor or the relevant authority."

### "Once on Demurrage" Rider
"Once the vessel is on demurrage, all time to count including Sundays, holidays, and time lost due to weather unless the vessel is in port or at berth for reasons within the shipowner's control."

### "SHEX on Demurrage" Rider (Charterer-Favorable)
"When on demurrage, Sundays and holidays shall be excepted." This overrides the standard "once on demurrage always on demurrage" principle and is advantageous to charterers.

### "Shifting Clause"
"Any shifting of the vessel at the request of the charterers within the port to be for charterers' time and expense. Shifting between ports to be for shipowners' account."

## Conflicting Clauses: Resolution Rules

When a printed clause and a rider clause conflict on laytime:
1. Rider clause prevails.
2. If two rider clauses conflict, the one added later (higher clause number or later date) prevails.
3. If truly irreconcilable, English law tends to give effect to both if possible (harmonious construction); if not, the clause most specifically addressing the point governs.
4. Ambiguity is construed against the party that drafted the clause (contra proferentem).

Always check the entire charter party — including annexes, amendments, and the recap — before drawing conclusions about the laytime regime.
