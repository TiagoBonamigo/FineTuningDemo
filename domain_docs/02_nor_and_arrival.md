# Notice of Readiness (NOR) and the Arrived Ship

## What Is a Notice of Readiness?

A Notice of Readiness (NOR) is a written declaration by the master of a vessel to the charterer (or their agent) that the vessel has arrived at the agreed destination and is ready in all respects — physically and legally — to commence loading or discharging operations. Tendering a valid NOR is the event that triggers the laytime countdown mechanism.

## Requirements for a Valid NOR

For an NOR to be valid, all of the following must typically be satisfied at the moment of tender:

1. **Arrived ship**: The vessel must have reached the contractual destination (port or berth, as specified).
2. **Physical readiness**: Cargo holds must be clean, dry, and ready; ship's equipment (cranes, pumps, hatch covers) must be in working order for the intended operation.
3. **Legal readiness**: All statutory certificates must be valid; the vessel must have obtained or be about to obtain free pratique (health clearance) and have completed inward customs formalities — unless WIFPON or WICCON clauses waive these requirements.
4. **Correct addressee**: NOR must be delivered to the entity specified in the charter party (typically the charterer or the charterer's port agent).
5. **Proper form**: Usually required in writing; an oral NOR may not be contractually valid unless the charter party permits it.
6. **Correct timing**: If the charter party restricts NOR tendering to "office hours" or "business hours", a NOR tendered outside those hours may not be valid or may only take effect at the start of the next business period.

## The "WIBON / WICCON / WIFPON" String

These acronyms modify the requirements for NOR validity and are almost always found together in the charter party:

### WIBON — Whether In Berth Or Not
The vessel may tender a valid NOR even if it has not yet secured a berth. This effectively converts a **berth charter party** into a **port charter party** for the purpose of laytime. Without WIBON, in a berth charter party, NOR cannot be given until the vessel is on the berth. With WIBON, waiting-for-berth time is transferred to the charterer's risk.

### WICCON — Whether In Custom Clearance Or Not
NOR is valid even if the vessel has not yet completed inward customs clearance or received customs entry. Without this clause, a vessel that has not cleared customs is not legally ready, and its NOR would be premature.

### WIFPON — Whether In Free Pratique Or Not
NOR is valid even if free pratique (the health clearance granted by port health authorities allowing a vessel to have contact with the shore) has not yet been granted. Without this clause, a vessel awaiting pratique is not legally ready, and its NOR would be invalid.

Together, the string "WIPON/WIBON/WICCON/WIFPON" creates the broadest possible window for NOR validity: the vessel can tender NOR even before reaching the port, before berthing, before clearing customs, and before obtaining health clearance.

### WIPON — Whether In Port Or Not
Even broader than WIBON. Allows NOR to be tendered before the vessel has even reached the legal port limits. Rarely seen alone; usually combined with WIBON.

## When Does NOR Become Effective?

The NOR is **tendered** at one time and becomes **effective** (i.e., starts the laytime clock ticking, subject to any turn time) at a potentially different time:

- If no WIBON and vessel is waiting in anchorage: NOR is premature and ineffective until the vessel berths.
- If WIBON applies: NOR is valid upon tender, and the time mechanism begins (subject to any notice period/turn time).
- If the charter party requires the charterer to "accept" the NOR: Laytime does not commence until acceptance, unless the charter party deems a deemed-acceptance after a certain period.
- Some charter parties provide that NOR tendered after office hours takes effect at the start of the next working day — regardless of WIBON.

## NOR Rejection and Protest

Charterers' agents sometimes "clause" or "reject" an NOR — endorsing it with conditions (e.g., "vessel not yet in berth", "free pratique not obtained"). Masters should note this, protest in writing, and seek legal guidance. A claused NOR does not necessarily become invalid; the legal effect depends on the specific clauses and the charter party terms.

If charterers wrongfully reject a valid NOR, laytime continues to run from when it should have commenced. The shipowner must keep a record of all correspondence regarding the NOR.

## Retroactive NOR

Some charter parties permit an NOR to be tendered retroactively — i.e., the master tenders it after the vessel has already been ready for some time, and the charter party backdates its effect to the actual time of readiness. This is uncommon and its applicability depends entirely on the charter party wording.

## Sequence of Events: From Arrival to Laytime Commencement

1. Vessel arrives at the contractual destination.
2. Master satisfies all conditions (ready physically and legally, or WICCON/WIFPON waives them).
3. Master tenders NOR in writing to charterers/agents.
4. NOR is received — time of receipt is recorded in the SOF.
5. Any turn time or notice period begins to run.
6. Laytime commences when the turn time / notice period expires (subject to SHEX and other clause restrictions on commencement timing).
